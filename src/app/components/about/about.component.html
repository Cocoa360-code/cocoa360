Our Story

Founded in 2015 by CEO Dr. Shadrack Frimpong, we employ community-centered development to help communities in Ghana thrive. Dr. Frimpong, the son of a cocoa farmer and a charcoal seller, he grew up without running water and electricity in rural Ghana. He became the first person from his village to attend college in the US, graduating from the University of Pennsylvania with the $150,000 President’s Engagement Prize (PEP), one of Penn’s highest honors. With the PEP, he pioneered the trailblazing community engagement model, farm-for-impact.

We facilitate the shift fromaid dependency to independent communities.
